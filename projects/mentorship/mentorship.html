Mentorship & Outreach Projects

Reaching out to inspire students in STEM

1. Mentorship Program

Structured technical education initiatives fostering robotics and programming skills among students and colleagues.

Project Overview

The Mentorship Program was a core component of knowledge transfer at RFL, involving structured technical instruction for diverse audiences. Daily sessions with the FGC team covered Arduino programming fundamentals, REV Robotics kit operation, sensor integration, and competition strategy. Individual mentorship was provided to students like Ryan, guiding them from basic concepts to hands-on robot assembly and operation. Additional workshops were conducted for staff members, introducing Arduino basics through practical exercises like LED blinking and servo control. This initiative developed skills in curriculum development, adaptive communication, and technical instruction while building internal capacity and fostering interest in STEM fields.

Key Features

  • Structured Curriculum: Developed progressive learning paths from basics to advanced concepts
  • Hands-On Learning: Practical exercises with Arduino and REV Robotics kits
  • Individualized Instruction: Tailored mentorship for students with varying skill levels
  • Staff Capacity Building: Workshops for non-technical colleagues to demystify electronics
  • Competition Preparation: Specialized training for FGC team members
  • Adaptive Communication: Developed ability to explain complex concepts to diverse audiences

2. Robotics Club Initiative

A strategic outreach program establishing robotics education in primary and secondary schools across Blantyre.

Project Overview

The Robotics Club Initiative which is being facilitated by Robotics Foundation Limited represents a strategic effort to expand STEM education by establishing robotics clubs in primary and secondary schools throughout Blantyre. The project has involved comprehensive planning, formal stakeholder communication, and direct community engagement. Responsibilities includes drafting official communication documents, conducting school visits to meet different administrators, and organizing robot showcases to generate student interest. A major showcase at Bedir Secondary School demonstrated various robotic systems and allowed students hands-on interaction, resulting in significant student registration for new clubs. This initiative provides valuable experience in project management, professional communication, stakeholder engagement, and public presentation while contributing to national STEM education goals.

Key Features

  • Strategic Planning: Developed comprehensive rollout strategy for multiple schools
  • Professional Communication: Drafted official letters and emails to school administrators
  • Stakeholder Engagement: Conducted meetings with headmasters to establish partnerships
  • Public Showcases: Organized interactive robot demonstrations to generate interest
  • Hands-On Learning: Enabled students to directly interact with robotic systems
  • Community Building: Established sustainable robotics education infrastructure in schools
