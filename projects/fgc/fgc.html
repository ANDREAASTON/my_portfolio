FGC Competition Robot

A high-performance competition robot designed for the First Global Challenge with advanced mechanical systems and sensor integration.

Project Overview

The FGC Competition Robot represented the pinnacle of technical work during the attachment, requiring integration of advanced mechanical design, sensor systems, and team leadership. The project began with redesigning a weak robot arm using chain and gear systems to achieve significant torque improvement through a 16-to-60 tooth gear ratio. The development process involved extensive CAD modeling in SolidWorks, virtual assembly simulation, and physical construction using REV Robotics kits. Beyond technical execution, the project included comprehensive mentorship of the FGC team, teaching block programming, sensor integration, and competition strategy. The final robot featured integrated REV touch and distance sensors, balanced weight distribution, and autonomous capabilities, ultimately being prepared for international competition in Panama.

Key Features

  • High-Torque Arm System: Redesigned with 16-to-60 tooth gear ratio for superior lifting capability
  • Advanced CAD Modeling: Comprehensive SolidWorks design with collision detection and motion simulation
  • Sensor Integration: Incorporated REV touch, distance, and color sensors for intelligent operation
  • Balanced Design: Optimized weight distribution for stable performance during competition maneuvers
  • Team Mentorship: Trained students in programming, strategy, and robot operation
  • Competition Ready: Fully tested, calibrated, and prepared for international competition
